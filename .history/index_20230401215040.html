Greetings! I am thrilled to share that I will start to pursue a PhD at Haas School of Business, UC Berkeley.

Currently, I am completing my undergraduate studies in Mathematics, Statistics, Economics, and Computational Science & Engineering at UIUC. During my time at UIUC, I am fortunately supervised by Prof. Song Lin and Prof. Ruta Mehta. Prior to my time at UIUC, I spent the first two years of my undergraduate studies at Zhejiang University in Hangzhou, China..

My research interests focus on the fields of Microeconomic Theory, Quantitative Marketing, and Behavioral Economics. I am particularly interested in applying economic theory to solve problems arising from information asymmetry on online platforms. In my latest independent working paper, I use rational inattention and Bayesian persuasion to study keyword design on online advertising platforms. In collaboration with Prof. Song Lin and Zijun Shi, I used game theory to investigate how royalty payments can be used to address adverse selection in NFT platforms. In the future, I hope to combine economic theory with experimental methods to study how asymmetric information and biased beliefs of consumers affect platforms and markets.
