About

I am a PhD Candidate in Marketing at the Haas School of Business, University of California, Berkeley. My research focuses on understanding consumer behavior in digital environments and the impact of technological innovations on marketing strategies.

Using a combination of econometric methods and experimental designs, I investigate how digital platforms shape consumer decision-making processes and market dynamics. My work aims to bridge the gap between theoretical marketing models and practical business applications.

Research

Primary Fields: Quantitative Marketing, Digital Platforms, Consumer Behavior

Digital Platform Design and Consumer Engagement

Job Market Paper

This research examines how platform design elements influence user engagement and retention. Through a series of field experiments and observational studies, I demonstrate that subtle changes in interface design can significantly impact user behavior and platform performance.

The Role of Social Influence in Digital Markets

Work in Progress

This project investigates how social influence mechanisms affect consumer choices in digital marketplaces. Using large-scale data analysis and controlled experiments, I explore the interaction between social proof, peer recommendations, and purchase decisions.
