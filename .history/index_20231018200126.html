(Strategically Disclosed) Ongoing Projects

    Fixing the Moral Blind Spot: Uncertainty-based Algorithm Design (with Xuhang Fan*)
  • Commercial services may inadvertently encourage immoral behaviors due to the inherent information asymmetry during implementation. For instance, online shopping platforms like Amazon allow consumers to report product losses and request refunds, but verifying such claims often poses significant challenges, leading to costly return fraud and insufficient regulation (Beneish et al., 2012). This research seeks to investigate the optimal regulation design when the deception of consumers remains largely imperceptible. Through a combination of analytical modeling and a lab experiment, we aim to construct an effective uncertainty-based reward-punishment mechanism that enhances ethical conduct under a return fraud context. Finally, a field experiment is designed to combat under-reporting at self-service checkouts in supermarkets (Gulino and Masera, 2023) and examine the potential of our proposed mechanisms in the real world.
