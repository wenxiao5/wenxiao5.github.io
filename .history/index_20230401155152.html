About Me

I am an undergraduate student in Mathematics, Statistics, Economics, and Computational Science & Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. I am fortunately supervised by Prof. Song Lin (HKUST marketing) and Prof. Ruta Mehta (UIUC computer science) during my undergraduate studies. Before coming to Illinois, I spent the first two years of my undergraduate study at Zhejiang University.

My research interests focus on the fields of Microeconomic Theory, Quantitative Marketing, and Behavioral Economics. I am particularly interested in applying economic theory to solve problems arising from information asymmetry on online platforms. In my latest independent working paper, I use rational inattention and Bayesian persuasion to study keyword design on online advertising platforms. In collaboration with Prof. Song Lin and Zijun Shi, I used game theory to investigate how royalty payments can be used to address adverse selection in NFT platforms. In the future, I hope to combine economic theory with experimental methods to study how consumers acquire information and how biased beliefs affect platforms and markets.

I am excited to announce that I will begin my PhD journey this summer at the Haas School of Business, University of California Berkeley!!!
